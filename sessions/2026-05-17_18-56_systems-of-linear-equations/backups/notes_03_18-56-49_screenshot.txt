## Topic
Linear systems, parametric solutions, row reduction

## Given Problem
Find the set of solutions for the linear system

$$-2x_1 - 6x_2 - 5x_3 = 12$$
$$6x_2 + 9x_3 = -9$$

Use s1, s2, etc. for the free variables if necessary.

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left( \quad , \quad , \quad \right)$$

## Augmented Matrix
Building the augmented matrix mechanically from the printed system:

Row 1: coefficients of $x_1, x_2, x_3$ from equation 1, then RHS:
$$[-2, -6, -5 \mid 12]$$

Row 2: coefficients of $x_1, x_2, x_3$ from equation 2, then RHS:
$$[0, 6, 9 \mid -9]$$

Augmented matrix:
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
-2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\
0 & 6 & 9 & -9
\end{array}\right]$$

## Work

**Step 1: Simplify Row 2**

Multiply Row 2 by $\frac{1}{3}$:
$$R_2 \to \frac{1}{3}R_2: \quad [0, 2, 3 \mid -3]$$

Matrix becomes:
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
-2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\
0 & 2 & 3 & -3
\end{array}\right]$$

**Step 2: Eliminate $x_2$ from Row 1**

Multiply Row 2 by 3:
$$3R_2: \quad [0, 6, 9 \mid -9]$$

Add to Row 1:
$$R_1 \to R_1 + 3R_2: \quad [-2, -6+6, -5+9 \mid 12-9] = [-2, 0, 4 \mid 3]$$

Matrix becomes:
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
-2 & 0 & 4 & 3 \\
0 & 2 & 3 & -3
\end{array}\right]$$

**Step 3: Scale to leading 1's**

$$R_1 \to -\frac{1}{2}R_1: \quad [1, 0, -2 \mid -\frac{3}{2}]$$

$$R_2 \to \frac{1}{2}R_2: \quad [0, 1, \frac{3}{2} \mid -\frac{3}{2}]$$

RREF:
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c}
1 & 0 & -2 & -\frac{3}{2} \\
0 & 1 & \frac{3}{2} & -\frac{3}{2}
\end{array}\right]$$

**Step 4: Interpret the solution**

From the RREF:
$$x_1 - 2x_3 = -\frac{3}{2}$$
$$x_2 + \frac{3}{2}x_3 = -\frac{3}{2}$$

Since we have 2 equations and 3 unknowns, $x_3$ is a free variable. Let $x_3 = s1$ (as instructed).

Then:
$$x_1 = -\frac{3}{2} + 2s1$$
$$x_2 = -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1$$
$$x_3 = s1$$

Parametric solution:
$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, s1\right)$$

Or equivalently:
$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right) + s1\left(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1\right)$$

## Check

Substitute into the original equations with $x_1 = -\frac{3}{2} + 2s1$, $x_2 = -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1$, $x_3 = s1$:

**Equation 1:** $-2x_1 - 6x_2 - 5x_3 = 12$
$$-2\left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1\right) - 6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) - 5(s1)$$
$$= 3 - 4s1 + 9 + 9s1 - 5s1$$
$$= 12 + 0s1 = 12 \quad \checkmark$$

**Equation 2:** $6x_2 + 9x_3 = -9$
$$6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) + 9(s1)$$
$$= -9 - 9s1 + 9s1$$
$$= -9 \quad \checkmark$$

## Final Answer

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, s1\right)$$

Or in vector form:
$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right) + s1(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1)$$

For WebWork entry format:
$$\boxed{\left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, \, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, \, s1\right)}$$
