Topic

Solving a system of three linear equations in two unknowns using Gaussian elimination (row reduction of augmented matrix).

Given Problem

Solve the system:

$$\left\{\begin{array}{rcl} x + y &=& -1 \\ 3x - 3y &=& 21 \\ 5x - 7y &=& 43 \end{array}\right.$$

If there is no solution, enter NONE in both answer blanks.

The answer fields show: $x = 3$, $y = -4$

Augmented Matrix

Building the augmented matrix mechanically from the printed system:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 3 & -3 & 21 \\ 5 & -7 & 43 \end{array}\right]$$

Work

Step 1: Eliminate the first column below the pivot.

$R_2 - 3R_1 \to R_2$:

$$R_2 = [3, -3, 21] - 3[1, 1, -1] = [3-3, -3-3, 21-(-3)] = [0, -6, 24]$$

$R_3 - 5R_1 \to R_3$:

$$R_3 = [5, -7, 43] - 5[1, 1, -1] = [5-5, -7-5, 43-(-5)] = [0, -12, 48]$$

Matrix becomes:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & -6 & 24 \\ 0 & -12 & 48 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 2: Eliminate the second column in row 3.

$R_3 - 2R_2 \to R_3$:

$$R_3 = [0, -12, 48] - 2[0, -6, 24] = [0, -12+12, 48-48] = [0, 0, 0]$$

Matrix becomes:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & -6 & 24 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 3: Simplify row 2.

$-\frac{1}{6}R_2 \to R_2$:

$$R_2 = [0, -6, 24] \cdot (-\frac{1}{6}) = [0, 1, -4]$$

Matrix becomes:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 4: Back-substitute to get reduced row echelon form.

$R_1 - R_2 \to R_1$:

$$R_1 = [1, 1, -1] - [0, 1, -4] = [1, 0, 3]$$

Final matrix:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

This gives us:

  • $x = 3$
  • $y = -4$

The third row $[0, 0, 0]$ represents $0 = 0$, which is always true. This means the system is consistent (it has a solution), and since we have two pivot positions for two variables, the solution is unique.

Check

Substitute $x = 3$ and $y = -4$ into the original printed equations:

Equation 1: $x + y = -1$

$$3 + (-4) = -1 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 2: $3x - 3y = 21$

$$3(3) - 3(-4) = 9 + 12 = 21 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 3: $5x - 7y = 43$

$$5(3) - 7(-4) = 15 + 28 = 43 \quad \checkmark$$

All three equations are satisfied.

Final Answer

$$x = 3, \quad y = -4$$

The values shown in the answer fields are correct.

Topic

Systems of Linear Equations (Row Operations / Elementary Matrices)

Given Problem

Week 1 Sections 1.2 1.3 and 1.4: Problem 5

Solve the system using row operations (or elementary matrices).

$$\begin{cases} 6x + 3y - 3z = 9 \\ 6x + 4y - 4z = 0 \\ 4x + 3y + 6z = 37 \end{cases}$$

Augmented Matrix

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 6 & 4 & -4 & 0 \\ 4 & 3 & 6 & 37 \end{array}\right]$$

Solution

Row Operation: $R_2 - R_1 \to R_2$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 4 & 3 & 6 & 37 \end{array}\right]$$

Row Operation: $3R_3 - 2R_1 \to R_3$

$$3R_3 - 2R_1: [12, 9, 18, 111] - [12, 6, -6, 18] = [0, 3, 24, 93]$$
$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 3 & 24 & 93 \end{array}\right]$$

Row Operation: $R_3 - 3R_2 \to R_3$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 0 & 27 & 120 \end{array}\right]$$

Back-substitution:

From row 3: $27z = 120$

$$z = \frac{120}{27} = \frac{40}{9}$$

From row 2: $y - z = -9$

$$y = -9 + \frac{40}{9} = \frac{-81 + 40}{9} = \frac{-41}{9}$$

From row 1: $6x + 3y - 3z = 9$

$$6x = 9 - 3y + 3z = 9 - 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) + 3\left(\frac{40}{9}\right)$$
$$6x = 9 + \frac{123}{9} + \frac{120}{9} = \frac{81 + 123 + 120}{9} = \frac{324}{9} = 36$$
$$x = 6$$

Final Answer

$$x = 6, \quad y = \frac{-41}{9}, \quad z = \frac{40}{9}$$

Verification

Substitute into the original equations:

Equation 1: $6(6) + 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) - 3\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 36 - \frac{123}{9} - \frac{120}{9} = 36 - 27 = 9$ ✓

Equation 2: $6(6) + 4\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) - 4\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 36 - \frac{164}{9} - \frac{160}{9} = 36 - 36 = 0$ ✓

Equation 3: $4(6) + 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) + 6\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 24 - \frac{123}{9} + \frac{240}{9} = 24 + 13 = 37$ ✓

Topic

Linear systems, parametric solutions, row reduction

Given Problem

Find the set of solutions for the linear system

$$-2x_1 - 6x_2 - 5x_3 = 12$$
$$6x_2 + 9x_3 = -9$$

Use s1, s2, etc. for the free variables if necessary.

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left( \quad , \quad , \quad \right)$$

Augmented Matrix

Building the augmented matrix mechanically from the printed system:

Row 1: coefficients of $x_1, x_2, x_3$ from equation 1, then RHS:

$$[-2, -6, -5 \mid 12]$$

Row 2: coefficients of $x_1, x_2, x_3$ from equation 2, then RHS:

$$[0, 6, 9 \mid -9]$$

Augmented matrix:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 & 9 & -9 \end{array}\right]$$

Work

Step 1: Simplify Row 2

Multiply Row 2 by $\frac{1}{3}$:

$$R_2 \to \frac{1}{3}R_2: \quad [0, 2, 3 \mid -3]$$

Matrix becomes:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & -3 \end{array}\right]$$

**Step 2: Eliminate $x_2$ from Row 1**

Multiply Row 2 by 3:

$$3R_2: \quad [0, 6, 9 \mid -9]$$

Add to Row 1:

$$R_1 \to R_1 + 3R_2: \quad [-2, -6+6, -5+9 \mid 12-9] = [-2, 0, 4 \mid 3]$$

Matrix becomes:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & -3 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 3: Scale to leading 1's

$$R_1 \to -\frac{1}{2}R_1: \quad [1, 0, -2 \mid -\frac{3}{2}]$$
$$R_2 \to \frac{1}{2}R_2: \quad [0, 1, \frac{3}{2} \mid -\frac{3}{2}]$$

RREF:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -\frac{3}{2} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{3}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} \end{array}\right]$$

Step 4: Interpret the solution

From the RREF:

$$x_1 - 2x_3 = -\frac{3}{2}$$
$$x_2 + \frac{3}{2}x_3 = -\frac{3}{2}$$

Since we have 2 equations and 3 unknowns, $x_3$ is a free variable. Let $x_3 = s1$ (as instructed).

Then:

$$x_1 = -\frac{3}{2} + 2s1$$
$$x_2 = -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1$$
$$x_3 = s1$$

Parametric solution:

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, s1\right)$$

Or equivalently:

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right) + s1\left(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1\right)$$

Check

Substitute into the original equations with $x_1 = -\frac{3}{2} + 2s1$, $x_2 = -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1$, $x_3 = s1$:

Equation 1: $-2x_1 - 6x_2 - 5x_3 = 12$

$$-2\left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1\right) - 6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) - 5(s1)$$
$$= 3 - 4s1 + 9 + 9s1 - 5s1$$
$$= 12 + 0s1 = 12 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 2: $6x_2 + 9x_3 = -9$

$$6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) + 9(s1)$$
$$= -9 - 9s1 + 9s1$$
$$= -9 \quad \checkmark$$

Final Answer

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, s1\right)$$

Or in vector form:

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right) + s1(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1)$$

For WebWork entry format:

$$\boxed{\left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, \, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, \, s1\right)}$$
