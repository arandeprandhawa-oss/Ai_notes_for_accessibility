Comprehensive Lecture Summary: Systems of Linear Equations and Row Operations

Main Topic and Learning Objectives

Topic: Solving Systems of Linear Equations using Row Operations and Elementary Matrices

Learning Objectives:

  • Convert systems of linear equations into augmented matrix form
  • Apply row operations (Gaussian elimination) to reduce matrices
  • Achieve row echelon form (REF) and reduced row echelon form (RREF)
  • Solve systems using back-substitution
  • Identify unique solutions, infinitely many solutions (parametric form), and inconsistent systems
  • Verify solutions by substituting back into original equations

Key Concepts

1. Augmented Matrix Representation

A system of linear equations can be represented as an augmented matrix, where the coefficient matrix is augmented with the constants from the right-hand side of the equations.

Example: The system

$$\begin{cases} 6x + 3y - 3z = 9 \\ 6x + 4y - 4z = 0 \\ 4x + 3y + 6z = 37 \end{cases}$$

becomes the augmented matrix

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 6 & 4 & -4 & 0 \\ 4 & 3 & 6 & 37 \end{array}\right]$$

2. Elementary Row Operations

Three types of elementary row operations preserve the solution set:

1. Row Replacement: Replace a row by the sum of itself and a multiple of another row

- Notation: $R_i + cR_j \to R_i$

2. Row Scaling: Multiply all entries in a row by a nonzero constant

- Notation: $cR_i \to R_i$

3. Row Interchange: Swap two rows

- Notation: $R_i \leftrightarrow R_j$

3. Row Echelon Form (REF) and Reduced Row Echelon Form (RREF)

Row Echelon Form properties:

  • All nonzero rows are above rows of all zeros
  • The leading entry (pivot) of each nonzero row is to the right of the leading entry of the row above it
  • All entries in a column below a leading entry are zeros

Reduced Row Echelon Form adds:

  • The leading entry in each nonzero row is 1
  • Each leading 1 is the only nonzero entry in its column

Problem 1: System with Unique Solution (3 equations, 3 unknowns)

Given System

$$\begin{cases} 6x + 3y - 3z = 9 \\ 6x + 4y - 4z = 0 \\ 4x + 3y + 6z = 37 \end{cases}$$

Step-by-Step Solution

Initial Augmented Matrix:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 6 & 4 & -4 & 0 \\ 4 & 3 & 6 & 37 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 1: $R_2 - R_1 \to R_2$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 4 & 3 & 6 & 37 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 2: $3R_3 - 2R_1 \to R_3$

Calculation: $[12, 9, 18, 111] - [12, 6, -6, 18] = [0, 3, 24, 93]$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 3 & 24 & 93 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 3: $R_3 - 3R_2 \to R_3$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 6 & 3 & -3 & 9 \\ 0 & 1 & -1 & -9 \\ 0 & 0 & 27 & 120 \end{array}\right]$$

Back-Substitution

From row 3: $27z = 120$

$$z = \frac{120}{27} = \frac{40}{9}$$

From row 2: $y - z = -9$

$$y = -9 + \frac{40}{9} = \frac{-81 + 40}{9} = \frac{-41}{9}$$

From row 1: $6x + 3y - 3z = 9$

$$6x = 9 - 3y + 3z = 9 - 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) + 3\left(\frac{40}{9}\right)$$
$$6x = 9 + \frac{123}{9} + \frac{120}{9} = \frac{81 + 123 + 120}{9} = \frac{324}{9} = 36$$
$$x = 6$$

Final Answer

$$x = 6, \quad y = \frac{-41}{9}, \quad z = \frac{40}{9}$$

Verification

Equation 1:

$$6(6) + 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) - 3\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 36 - \frac{123}{9} - \frac{120}{9} = 36 - 27 = 9 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 2:

$$6(6) + 4\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) - 4\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 36 - \frac{164}{9} - \frac{160}{9} = 36 - 36 = 0 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 3:

$$4(6) + 3\left(\frac{-41}{9}\right) + 6\left(\frac{40}{9}\right) = 24 - \frac{123}{9} + \frac{240}{9} = 24 + 13 = 37 \quad \checkmark$$

Problem 2: System with Unique Solution (3 equations, 2 unknowns)

Given System

$$\begin{cases} x + y = -1 \\ 3x - 3y = 21 \\ 5x - 7y = 43 \end{cases}$$

Step-by-Step Solution

Initial Augmented Matrix:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 3 & -3 & 21 \\ 5 & -7 & 43 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 1: Eliminate first column below pivot

$R_2 - 3R_1 \to R_2$: $[3, -3, 21] - 3[1, 1, -1] = [0, -6, 24]$

$R_3 - 5R_1 \to R_3$: $[5, -7, 43] - 5[1, 1, -1] = [0, -12, 48]$

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & -6 & 24 \\ 0 & -12 & 48 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 2: Eliminate second column in row 3

$R_3 - 2R_2 \to R_3$: $[0, -12, 48] - 2[0, -6, 24] = [0, 0, 0]$

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & -6 & 24 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 3: Simplify row 2

$-\frac{1}{6}R_2 \to R_2$:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 1 & -1 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 4: Back-substitute to get RREF

$R_1 - R_2 \to R_1$:

$$\left[\begin{array}{cc|c} 1 & 0 & 3 \\ 0 & 1 & -4 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 \end{array}\right]$$

Final Answer

$$x = 3, \quad y = -4$$

Interpretation: The third row $[0, 0, 0]$ represents $0 = 0$ (always true), indicating the system is consistent. Since we have 2 pivot positions for 2 variables, the solution is unique. This means the third equation is a linear combination of the first two.

Verification

Equation 1: $3 + (-4) = -1$ ✓

Equation 2: $3(3) - 3(-4) = 9 + 12 = 21$ ✓

Equation 3: $5(3) - 7(-4) = 15 + 28 = 43$ ✓

Problem 3: System with Infinitely Many Solutions (Parametric Form)

Given System

$$\begin{cases} -2x_1 - 6x_2 - 5x_3 = 12 \\ 6x_2 + 9x_3 = -9 \end{cases}$$

Step-by-Step Solution

Initial Augmented Matrix:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\ 0 & 6 & 9 & -9 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 1: Simplify Row 2

$\frac{1}{3}R_2 \to R_2$:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & -6 & -5 & 12 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & -3 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 2: Eliminate $x_2$ from Row 1

$R_1 + 3R_2 \to R_1$: $[-2, -6+6, -5+9 \mid 12-9] = [-2, 0, 4 \mid 3]$

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} -2 & 0 & 4 & 3 \\ 0 & 2 & 3 & -3 \end{array}\right]$$

Step 3: Scale to leading 1's

$-\frac{1}{2}R_1 \to R_1$ and $\frac{1}{2}R_2 \to R_2$:

$$\left[\begin{array}{ccc|c} 1 & 0 & -2 & -\frac{3}{2} \\ 0 & 1 & \frac{3}{2} & -\frac{3}{2} \end{array}\right]$$

Interpreting the Solution

From the RREF:

$$\begin{cases} x_1 - 2x_3 = -\frac{3}{2} \\ x_2 + \frac{3}{2}x_3 = -\frac{3}{2} \end{cases}$$

Since we have 2 equations and 3 unknowns, $x_3$ is a free variable. Let $x_3 = s1$.

Parametric Solution:

$$x_1 = -\frac{3}{2} + 2s1$$
$$x_2 = -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1$$
$$x_3 = s1$$

Final Answer

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1, \, -\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1, \, s1\right)$$

Vector Form:

$$(x_1, x_2, x_3) = \left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right) + s1\left(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1\right)$$

This represents a line in 3D space where:

  • The point $\left(-\frac{3}{2}, -\frac{3}{2}, 0\right)$ is a particular solution
  • The vector $\left(2, -\frac{3}{2}, 1\right)$ is the direction vector

Verification

Equation 1:

$$-2\left(-\frac{3}{2} + 2s1\right) - 6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) - 5(s1) = 3 - 4s1 + 9 + 9s1 - 5s1 = 12 \quad \checkmark$$

Equation 2:

$$6\left(-\frac{3}{2} - \frac{3}{2}s1\right) + 9(s1) = -9 - 9s1 + 9s1 = -9 \quad \checkmark$$

Major Conclusions and Takeaways

Types of Solution Sets

1. Unique Solution: The system has exactly one solution (number of pivots = number of variables)

- Example: Problems 1 and 2

2. Infinitely Many Solutions: The system has free variables (number of pivots < number of variables)

- Example: Problem 3

- Solutions expressed in parametric form using free variables

3. No Solution: The system is inconsistent (a row reduces to $[0 \, 0 \, \cdots \, 0 \mid b]$ where $b \neq 0$)

- Not illustrated in these examples

Key Strategic Points

1. Choose efficient row operations: Look for opportunities to create zeros efficiently (like using combinations of rows)

2. Work systematically: Eliminate columns from left to right, creating pivots

3. Always verify: Substitute final answers back into original equations

4. Free variables: When there are more variables than pivots, the remaining variables are free parameters

5. RREF advantages: Reduced row echelon form makes reading off solutions immediate, but REF with back-substitution is often faster

Pivot Analysis

The number and position of pivots determines:

  • Whether a system is consistent
  • Whether the solution is unique or has infinitely many solutions
  • Which variables are free (those without pivots)

Questions and Answers

Q1: When should I use back-substitution versus full RREF?

A: Back-substitution from REF is typically faster for finding solutions. Full RREF is useful when you need to clearly identify free variables or when the problem specifically requires it.

---

Q2: How do I know if a system is consistent before completing row reduction?

A: You cannot determine consistency in advance. Complete the row reduction. If you obtain a row of the form $[0 \, 0 \, \cdots \, 0 \mid b]$ with $b \neq 0$, the system is inconsistent.

---

Q3: What does it mean geometrically when we have a free variable?

A: Each free variable adds a dimension to the solution set:

  • One free variable → solution is a line
  • Two free variables → solution is a plane
  • And so on

---

Q4: In Problem 2, why did the third row become all zeros?

A: This indicates the third equation was linearly dependent on the first two equations. Geometrically, the three planes intersect at a single point, but the third plane passes through the intersection of the first two.

---

Q5: How do I check my work efficiently?

A: Always substitute your final answer(s) back into the original equations, not the reduced matrix. This catches any arithmetic errors made during row reduction.
